Policy 2: Customer Support Service Level Policy (CS-SLA-002)

Effective Date: January 1, 2027
 Owner: Customer Success Department

The purpose of this policy is to establish customer support service standards and define measurable response and resolution objectives to ensure timely, effective, and consistent support for customers.

Customer support teams are required to meet specific service-level targets based on ticket priority. Critical issues must receive an initial response within 15 minutes and be resolved within 4 hours. High-priority tickets must receive an initial response within 1 hour and be resolved within 8 hours. Medium-priority tickets must receive an initial response within 4 hours and be resolved within 24 hours. Low-priority tickets must receive an initial response within one business day and be resolved within three business days.

To maintain service excellence, support teams are expected to achieve a first-response SLA compliance rate of at least 98% and a resolution SLA compliance rate of at least 95%. Customer Satisfaction Scores (CSAT) must average 4.5 out of 5.0 or higher. In addition, the escalation rate must remain below 10%, and the ticket reopen rate must remain below 5%.

Appropriate staffing levels are required to support these objectives. The organization will maintain a minimum coverage ratio of one support analyst for every 250 active customers. Support queues must be monitored at intervals no greater than 15 minutes, and 24x7 after-hours coverage must be provided for all Critical incidents.

To ensure consistent service quality, each support analyst must undergo a minimum of 10 ticket audits per month, maintain a quality score of at least 90%, and complete all required training programs, resulting in a training completion rate of 100%.

Support performance will be monitored through dashboards that are reviewed monthly by department leadership. These reviews will assess compliance with established service standards and identify opportunities for improvement.

If performance falls below acceptable levels, corrective action plans will be required. Remediation measures must be implemented whenever the Customer Satisfaction Score falls below 4.2, SLA compliance drops below 90%, or analyst quality scores remain below 85% for two consecutive months.
